AI and how I use it

I use AI as a productivity tool to accelerate learning and improve the quality of my work, especially when I am writing code, troubleshooting, or turning lab notes into clear documentation. I treat AI like a junior pair programmer: helpful for drafts and ideas, but never a replacement for verification.

How it helps me

  • Generate first pass code scaffolds and then refine them to match requirements
  • Debug faster by asking for likely root causes and targeted checks
  • Translate messy notes into clean recruiter friendly writeups
  • Practice explaining technical concepts in plain language

My workflow

  • 1) Define constraints what the page or tool must do and what done looks like
  • 2) Draft ask for a starting point for structure, functions, or wording
  • 3) Verify test in the browser or CLI and validate results against requirements
  • 4) Improve refactor for readability, accessibility, and maintainability
  • 5) Document summarize decisions, steps, and what I learned

Quality standards

  • I review AI output line by line and I do not copy and paste blindly
  • I prefer simple readable solutions
  • I focus on semantic HTML, clear headings, and keyboard navigation
  • I keep naming consistent and add comments when it improves clarity

Security and ethics

  • I do not paste secrets or private credentials into prompts
  • I use AI for learning and defensive security, not for misuse
  • I validate security guidance against documentation and lab results
  • I treat suggestions as hypotheses until tested

Examples in this portfolio

  • Turning coursework into a structured website with clear navigation and artifact pages
  • Writing concise summaries for labs so a recruiter can understand them quickly
  • Improving wording and formatting to match professional expectations

Bottom line

AI helps me move faster, but I am responsible for the final result. My process stays the same: understand the problem, build a solution, verify it, and document it.
